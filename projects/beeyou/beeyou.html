background:

BEEYOU was an individual project
assignment for a student run course
at UC Berkeley that focused on
graphic design and branding.
The goal of the course was to
ultimately come up with a brand
concept for a self-made company.

concept:

BEEYOU is a care-package brand
that creates uniquely themed
packaging and items that revolve
around any emotion its users
may have.

By creating a care-package for
all occasions, happy or not, BEEYOU
strives to normalize emotions
in a world where it may not be
easy to do so.
You and the people you love
deserve a treat no matter what
you’re feeling, and BEEYOU is
here for you.

BEEYOU was designed with a soft,
friendly yet vibrant atmosphere
in mind. The logo uses a pictorial
mark instead of the classic
letter-mark or combination logo:
the result was not only a memorable
mascot with an emotional aspect,
but also a play on the letter B.

At first, I had drafted a few pastel
palettes, but ended up going with
slightly more saturated colors to
represent the variety of emotions
and also for more visual accessibility.
I also chose tomake assets that built
on each other, including finishing
sentences or including similar
captions to keep brand cohesiveness
and connect each emotion
with each other.

reflection:

I created BEEYOU during the height of Covid-19 where emotions were running high, people couldn't see each other for months, and mental health was more important than ever. Personally, I was struggling a lot with the death of my beloved grandparents. BEEYOU was a reflection of my growth to accept that it was okay that I wasn’t okay. Due to the situation, I regret I wasn’t able to put more time into this project so I’d like to
revisit it someday.
